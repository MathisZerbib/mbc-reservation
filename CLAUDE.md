## Design Context

### Users
High-end restaurant patrons looking for a seamless, premium booking experience for a Canadian professional brasserie. The interface should reflect the quality of the craft beers and professional service.

### Brand Personality
**Professional, Craft, Premium.**
The brand balances the rugged, authentic nature of a Canadian brasserie with the sophisticated service of a high-end establishment.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual Tone**: Clean, high-end, and modern. 
- **Theme**: Primarily Light Mode (Slate/White/Indigo) to maintain a professional, airy feel, though dark elements can be used for deep-contrast "hero" moments.
- **Palette**: Slate neutrals for depth, Indigo-600 for primary actions, and Emerald-600 for success/confirmation.
- **Typography & Form**: Heavy emphasis on `font-black` for hierarchy, `rounded-3xl` for a modern "app-like" feel, and hardware-accelerated animations (Framer Motion).

### Design Principles
1. **Premium Precision**: Every interaction must feel snappy and deliberate (use Spring physics, avoid linear transitions).
2. **Brasserie Authenticity**: Use clean lines and high-quality spacing to reflect professional "brasserie" standards—nothing should feel cluttered.
3. **Frictionless Utility**: Patrons are here for the beer and food; the booking process must be the shortest path between intent and confirmation.
4. **Visual Credibility**: Use subtle glows (`box-glow`) and backdrop blurs to signify a modern, state-of-the-art service.
